Beyond Academic Training

Beyond my academic training in forensic genetics, I have worked as a software tester in life science companies, e.g. Biocrates, focusing on metabolomic research for personalized medicine, and MED-EL, a pioneer in cochlear implants. My research has focused on developing reproducible frameworks and public databases. These experiences have taught me to work with complex datasets, prioritize reproducibility, and understand how scientific rigor translates into tools that matter and are accessible to non-scientists.

From Forensics to Population Health
🔀

Redirecting Expertise

Now I want to redirect my expertise toward understanding living populations and their health trajectories. The complexity of modern health - how diseases accumulate, how early interventions might prevent trajectories - are questions that need rigorous analysis and may help solve problems our society is currently facing. I am eager to dive into that work with your team.

Research Fit
🎯

Healthy Aging & Early Identification

I am particularly drawn to your goal of promoting healthy aging through early disease identification. My strength lies in analyzing large, complex datasets and stratifying populations to identify subgroup-specific patterns. This mirrors phylogenetic approaches: just as evolutionary relationships become visible across population structure, health trajectories likely contain meaningful variation across subpopulations. Bringing this lens to your work on personalized healthcare pathways could help identify actionable intervention points.
